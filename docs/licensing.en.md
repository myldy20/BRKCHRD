In practical terms, you may run, study, copy and modify the program. When you distribute the program or a derivative, the corresponding source and the same GPL freedoms must be provided as required by the license.

## Origin notice

BRKCHRD adds a reasonable origin notice under GNU GPLv3 section 7(b). A redistributed modified version must preserve the notice from [`NOTICE.md`](../NOTICE.md):

> Based on BRKCHRD by Myldy design — https://github.com/myldy20/BRKCHRD

Place it in the source distribution and in an About, Credits, documentation or other normally visible attribution location when such a location exists. A source-only derivative may keep it in its README and source tree.

## What the notice does not require

- it does not require using the BRKCHRD name for a fork;
- it does not require using the project logo or artwork;
- it does not prevent commercial use allowed by GPLv3;
- it does not imply endorsement by Myldy design;
- it does not allow a modified build to present itself as an official BRKCHRD release.

Forks should use distinct names and visual identities.

## Artwork and identity

The logo, banner, PortMaster cover and NextUI card image are official project identity assets. They are included to distribute and identify the unmodified BRKCHRD project. Do not reuse them to brand a modified or unrelated product in a way that creates confusion about origin or endorsement.

## Third-party components

See [`THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`](../THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md) for libraries, references and their licenses. A distribution must preserve all applicable third-party notices in addition to BRKCHRD's GPL and origin notice.
